1.	Software Requirements Specifications (SRS):
A software requirement specification (SRS) includes information about all the functional and non-functional requirements for a given piece of software. The SRS serves as the main point of reference for the software development team who’ll build the software product, as well as for all other involved stakeholders.
`	1.1 Purpose:
	The purpose of SRS is to:
•	To define the complete scope and objectives of the AutoRent project clearly.
•	To establish a mutual understanding between developers, clients, and stakeholders about system functions and features.
•	To serve as a reference document for system design, development, and testing.
•	To specify functional and non-functional requirements in a structured format.
•	To help project managers plan and estimate time, cost, and resources effectively.
•	To provide a basis for validation and verification, ensuring the final system meets user expectations.
•	To identify potential risks and dependencies early in the project lifecycle.
•	To act as a guide for future maintenance and enhancements after system deployment.
•	To ensure consistent communication among all project participants (developers, testers, clients, and supervisors).

1.3	Scope:
AutoRent aims to digitalize the vehicle rental process in Nepal by connecting renters and vehicle owners through a unified online platform. The system supports both car and bike rentals, online payments, real-time chat, map-based garage assistance, location of vechile and AI chatbot guidance.
The platform ensures transparency, trust, and convenience in vehicle renting while supporting digital identity verification and secure financial transactions.
Key objectives:
•	Enable renters to book cars and bikes online.
•	Allow vehicle owners to manage listings and bookings.
•	Integrate secure payment gateways (eSewa, Khalti, Stripe).
•	Offer 24/7 AI chatbot support and real-time communication.
•	Provide a GPS-based garage locator for emergencies and vechile location for renting.

5.	Assumptions and Dependencies
•	Users have stable internet and basic digital literacy.
•	APIs (Maps, Payments) remain active and licensed.
•	Owners upload legitimate vehicle documents.
•	Server hosting is scalable and secure.

6.	Database Requirements:
•	Database: PostgreSQL (Relational).
•	Integrity: Enforce ACID compliance.
•	Backup: Weekly backups with recovery option.
•	Data Retention: Archive old data after 12 months.
•	Optimization: Use indexing for faster queries.






1.7	Other Requirements:
•	Legal Requirements
1)	Must comply with Nepal’s Data Protection Act (2022) and related cyber security laws.
2)	User consent and privacy policies must follow ethical and legal data-handling standards.
3)	All uploaded documents (ID, license) must be securely encrypted and stored.
4)	Financial transactions must comply with the rules of payment gateways (eSewa, Khalti, Stripe).
5)	Intellectual property rights of system design, source code, and AI modules belong to the AutoRent development team.

•	Hardware and Environmental Requirements
1)	Server Specs: Minimum 4-core CPU, 8 GB RAM, 100 GB SSD storage.
2)	Client Devices: Compatible with desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones with modern browsers.
3)	Power & Security: Host servers in a secure, monitored data center with 24/7 uptime and backup power.
4)	Deployment Environment: Cloud-based hosting using Render, Vercel, or AWS.

•	Third-Party Integrations
1)	Map API: Google Maps API for real-time garage locator,navigation and vechile location.
2)	Payment Gateways: eSewa, Khalti, and Stripe for online transactions.
3)	Cloud Storage: Cloudinary or Firebase for storing user and vehicle images.
4)	Notification Services: Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) for alerts and confirmations.
5)	AI Chatbot Framework: Flask or Rasa or any other integrated with the backend API.
