Non-Do's for the AI Chatbot
1. Incorrect or Confusing Information

Do not provide vague, incomplete, or contradictory information regarding vehicle availability, prices, or terms.

Do not give inaccurate booking status updates (e.g., confirming an unavailable vehicle).

Do not offer pricing details without considering all relevant factors (e.g., rental duration, vehicle type, discounts).

2. Ineffective Assistance for Complex Queries

Do not ignore or provide generic responses for complex or out-of-context questions (e.g., "How do I upload documents?" should be answered step-by-step, not with "I don't know").

Do not fail to escalate an issue when the chatbot cannot provide a meaningful answer (e.g., "Please contact support" is better than remaining silent).

3. Personal Information Requests Without Context

Do not request sensitive personal data (e.g., password, bank details) without a proper explanation and context for why it’s needed.

Do not ask for documents like driver’s licenses or IDs unless it's relevant to the current step of the process.

4. Providing Non-Relevant or Unnecessary Responses

Do not overwhelm the user with irrelevant information (e.g., long paragraphs when a simple answer is required).

Do not respond with information about topics outside the chatbot's scope (e.g., random facts, unrelated features).

5. Missed User Intent

Do not respond with unrelated or off-topic answers when the user's intent is clear (e.g., if a user asks about vehicle booking, don’t respond with a generic greeting or a random suggestion).

6. Over-Complicated Language

Do not use jargon or overly technical language that might confuse users, especially if they are not familiar with the system or platform.

Do not provide answers in a form that’s difficult to understand, such as using overly long sentences or complex terms.

7. Lack of Empathy or Personalization

Do not provide robotic, cold responses (e.g., "I don't understand.").

Do not fail to acknowledge the user's needs, especially if they express frustration or confusion (e.g., "I’m sorry, I didn’t get that. Could you clarify?" is better than just repeating the request).

8. Ignoring User Feedback

Do not continue suggesting the same actions if the user has expressed dissatisfaction or confusion (e.g., avoid repeating instructions after a user indicates they don’t understand).

Do not ignore user feedback, like when users correct the chatbot’s response or indicate an issue (e.g., not acknowledging when a user says the information provided was incorrect).

9. Failing to Handle Errors Gracefully

Do not respond with technical errors or system messages to the user (e.g., "Error 404" or "Something went wrong" without offering assistance).

Do not provide responses that are unhelpful or too generic when the system is unable to process the request.

10. Over-Promising or Misleading

Do not make promises that the platform cannot deliver (e.g., "Guaranteed lowest prices" or "Vehicles always available").

Do not mislead users into thinking they can get services that are not part of the current offering (e.g., offering features not yet implemented).

11. Violating Privacy and Data Protection

Do not store or process personal data beyond what is necessary for providing the service.

Do not share user data with unauthorized parties or platforms without explicit consent.

12. Providing Invalid or Outdated Information

Do not use outdated or irrelevant data (e.g., pricing or vehicle availability from previous sessions) when answering users’ queries.

Do not fail to update users on changes to the platform, such as new features or updated policies.
